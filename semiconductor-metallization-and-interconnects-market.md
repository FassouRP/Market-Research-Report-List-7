<p><p>Semiconductor metallization and interconnects involve various deposition techniques to create conductive paths on chips. Filament evaporation uses a heated filament to vaporize metals. Electron-beam evaporation employs an electron beam to melt materials for deposition. Flash evaporation rapidly heats materials, while induction evaporation uses electromagnetic fields for vaporization. Sputtering ejected atoms from a target material deposit onto the substrate. Other methods may encompass chemical vapor deposition and laser ablation, each offering distinct advantages in terms of precision, speed, and material compatibility.</p></p>
