<p><ul><li>Microwave Sensors</li><li>Infrared Time-of-Flight sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Occupancy monitoring involves various sensor technologies for detecting presence in spaces. Microwave sensors utilize electromagnetic waves to identify movement and occupancy, offering high sensitivity and range. Infrared Time-of-Flight sensors measure the time taken by infrared light to bounce back, enabling precise occupancy detection. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves, effectively sensing motion and presence, even in complex environments. Other market types may include passive infrared sensors, light sensors, and thermal imaging, each providing unique advantages for efficient space utilization and energy management.</p></p>
