<p><ul><li>Maxam-Gilbert Sequencing</li><li>Chain-Termination Methods</li></ul></p>
<p><p>DNA sequencing technologies can be classified into two primary market types: Maxam-Gilbert Sequencing and Chain-Termination Methods. Maxam-Gilbert sequencing employs chemical cleavage of DNA at specific bases, which allows for the identification of nucleotide sequences but is less commonly used due to its complexity and safety concerns. Chain-termination methods, including Sanger sequencing, utilize dideoxynucleotides to terminate DNA strand elongation, making it more popular due to its accuracy and ease of use, particularly in various genomic applications.</p></p>
