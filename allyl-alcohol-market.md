The allyl acetate hydrolysis method involves the hydrolysis of allyl acetate to yield allyl alcohol, typically favored for its straightforward process and efficiency. Conversely, the PO isomerization method converts propylene oxide into allyl alcohol, characterized by high selectivity and yield. Both methods cater to the growing demand in various applications, including pharmaceuticals, resins, and coatings.</p></p>
