<p><p>The Universal Power Line Carrier (UPLC) market is divided into two main types: low-voltage UPLC and high-voltage UPLC. Low-voltage UPLC is used for communication and control in residential and commercial applications, typically operating below 1 kV. In contrast, high-voltage UPLC is utilized in transmission networks, functioning above 1 kV to manage grid operations and ensure reliable energy distribution. Both types facilitate data transmission over power lines, enhancing utility management and smart grid functionalities while reducing infrastructure costs.</p></p>
