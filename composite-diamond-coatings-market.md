<p><p>Composite diamond coatings are advanced materials applied to various substrates to enhance their performance. The market is primarily segmented into two types: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD). CVD involves chemical reactions that deposit diamond layers on surfaces, resulting in superior hardness and wear resistance. PVD, on the other hand, utilizes physical processes like vaporization to create thin diamond films. Both methods are critical for industries requiring durable tools, cutting instruments, and protective coatings.</p></p>
