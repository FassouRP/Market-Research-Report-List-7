<p><p>The nanofiber materials market encompasses three main types: metal-based, ceramic-based, and polymer-based nanofibers. Metal-based nanofibers, often used for their electrical and thermal conductivity, find applications in electronics and sensors. Ceramic-based nanofibers are valued for their thermal stability and strength, making them suitable for filtration and insulation. Polymer-based nanofibers, widely used due to their versatility and ease of production, serve diverse applications in textiles, biomedical devices, and environmental remediation, each offering unique properties and benefits.</p></p>
