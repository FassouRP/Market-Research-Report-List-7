<p><p>The semiconductor ceramic packaging materials market includes various types of substrates tailored for specific applications. HTCC (High-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic) is known for its excellent thermal stability, ideal for high-performance electronics. LTCC (Low-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic) enables multilayer designs with enhanced integration. DBC (Direct Bonded Copper) substrates provide superior electrical and thermal conductivity. AMB (Alumina-Magnesium-Boric) substrates are suitable for high-frequency applications. DPC (Direct Plated Copper) allows for direct attachments, while DBA (Direct Bonded Aluminum) substrates excel in high-power applications, enhancing device reliability and efficiency.</p></p>
