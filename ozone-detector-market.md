<p><p>The ozone detector market includes various technologies such as ultraviolet photometers and electrochemical sensors. Ultraviolet photometers measure ozone concentrations by assessing the absorption of UV light at specific wavelengths, providing high accuracy and rapid results. In contrast, electrochemical sensors detect ozone through redox reactions involving gas molecules and electrodes, offering a cost-effective and compact solution. Both types serve diverse applications, including environmental monitoring, industrial safety, and public health, each meeting specific regulatory and operational needs.</p></p>
